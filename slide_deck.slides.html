Investigation Overview¶

This project is an exploration into trends in loan data from Prosper. The primary factors of interest will be credit ratings and APRs for loans. In particular there will be an examination of changes in loans before and after July 2009, where credit ratings shifted from the variabel CreditGrades to ProsperRatings, and the effect of this on trends in APRs and the proportion of loans for homeowners to non-homeowners.

Dataset Overview¶

The data set provided holds information for 113,937 loans with 81 variables for each loan. This includes informatino such as loan amount, borrower rate, the status of the loan, and other information abou both the loan and borrower.

The first visualization shows a whiskered bar chart of borrower APRs by credit ratings. Here a relationship is seen where lower ratings tend to have lower APRs, suggesting lower risks and therefore lower fees. Interestingly loans marked as 'NC' have APR's around that of 'C' or 'B' loans, which are also the most common loans in the dataset.

In the second visualization, two clustered bar charts showing the counts of loans broken down by both credit ratings and whether the loan is for a homeowner or non-homeowner are further divided into pre-2009 CreditGrade loans and post-2009 ProsperRating loans. While in both datasets it is generally more likely for a loan with a higher rating to be for a homeowner ompared to a non-homeowner, with the shift to non-homeowner preference occuring at the 'C' rating, there is a notable difference in the pre-2009 data. There, there is a growth in overall numbers of loans going down in credit ratings for only non-homeowners, and a growth in the overall number of loans going up in credit ratings for only homeowners, which isn't seen in the post-2009 data or in the overall dataset.
